Biography

Jiang obtained his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln in 2018. He currently works as Research Assistant in Systems Biology and Biomedical Informatics (SBBI) Laboratory under the supervision of Dr. Juan Cui. Before joining SBBI, he received his B.S. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from Huazhong Normal University (Wuhan, China) in 2007 and M.S. in Statistics from University of Nebraska-Lincoln in 2013.

His primary research interests are in machine learning, data mining, predictive modeling, dynamic networks and data integration. His doctoral research focuses on the developments of new data-driven apporaches for the biological problems, including the gene regulatory networks during disease progression, non-coding RNA regulation, extracellular RNAs detection, etc.

A systematic approach to RNA-associated motif discovery

In this work, we have demonstrated the promising performance of a new motif discovery approach that is particularly effective in current RNA applications. Important discoveries resulting from this work include the identification of possible RNA-loading motifs in a variety of exosomes, as well as novel insights in sequence features of RNA cargos, i.e., short non-coding RNAs and mRNAs may share similar loading mechanism into exosomes. This method has been implemented and deployed as a new webserver named MDS2, which is accessible at http://sbbi-panda.unl.edu/MDS2/, along with a standalone package available for download at https://github.com/sbbi/MDS2.
Gao T#, Shu J#, Cui J
In BMC Genomics, 2018
Code Webserver

Dynamic and modularized microRNA regulation and its implication in human cancers

A new integrative method to study the dynamic process of microRNA regulation by considering both competing and cooperative mechanisms and identifying functional modules where different microRNAs co-regulate the same functional process. Specifically, a new pipeline was built based on a meta-Lasso regression model and the proof-of-concept study was performed using a large-scale genomic dataset from ~4,200 patients with 9 cancer types. In the analysis, 10,726 microRNA-mRNA interactions were identified to be associated with a specific stage and/or type of cancer, which demonstrated the dynamic and conditional miRNA regulation during cancer progression.
Shu J, Vieira RSB, Gao T, Xu Z, Cui J
In Scientific Reports, 2017
Code Journal Database

Computational characterization of exogenous microRNAs that can be transferred into human circulation

An integrated genomics and computational analysis to study the potential deciding features of cross-species transportable microRNAs. Specifically, we analyzed all publicly available microRNAs, a total of 34,612 from 194 species, with 1,102 features derived from the microRNA sequence and structure. Through in-depth bioinformatics analysis, 8 groups of discriminative features have been used to characterize human circulating microRNAs and infer the likelihood that a microRNA will get transferred into human circulation.
Shu J, Chiang K, Zempleni J, Cui J
In PLoS ONE, 2015
